Have you started your business?

If you’re just thinking about starting a partnership, you’re not eligible for an ABN.

To be eligible for an ABN, we expect you to have taken several steps to starting a partnership to show you’re committed, such as:

  • meet with financial or business advisors
  • write a business plan
  • estimate your potential business profit
  • apply for finance
  • run feasibility studies
  • lease or buy premises or equipment for the partnership
  • obtain licences to operate
  • advertise your partnership.

One-off business deal

If you’re making one-off business deals or sales to make a large profit or gain (such as selling property), then you’ll need an ABN.

If you expect to make a loss, or a small profit, you don’t need to have an ABN because you won’t need to report it.

Sell products or services

When we say:

  • “sell products or services”, we include renting or leasing out property such as land, cars, boats, copyrights and patents.
  • “regularly”, we mean repeatedly. For example, if you plan to sell your products at market stores every 4 to 6 weeks.
  • “continuously”, we mean non-stop. For example, if you earn a continuous income from renting out farming land to one tenant for the next 20 years.

If your business activity isn’t ongoing or repeated, or you’re not planning to make a profit or gain, what you’re doing could be more of a hobby than a business. Hobbies don’t need ABNs. For more about this, read the Tax Office’s Business or hobby? page.
